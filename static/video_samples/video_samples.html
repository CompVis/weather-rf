Probabilistic Weather Nowcasting with Rectified Flow Transformers

1. Comparison to previous work

Our forecast aligns more closely with the ground truth than CasCast and Earthformer. Earthformer forecasts become blurry over time due to the deterministic training objective. In CasCast extreme precipitation is heavily oversampled and temporal consistency is impaired (see right-most example). Our forecasts exhibit high temporal consistency through united decoding and remain sharp.

2. Forecasts

Forecast ensemble members are sharp and show high temporal consistency. All ensemble members are perceptually close to the ground truth but exhibit variability in details especially in high precipitation regions.

For rare storm events such as hurricanes our model is able to capture the circular motion and produce diverse yet realistic forecasts.

3. Reconstructions

FREUD reconstruction ensemble members show high similarity and temporal consistency. variability in details is observed especially in high precipitation regions. All ensemble members match the ground truth well.

4. Frame-wise vs. united decoding

Frame-wise DiffAE vs. FREUD reconstructions. Both variants match the ground truth well. However, the frame-wise decoding shows impaired temporal consistency especially in high precipitation regions.
